Line Charts

Most suitable for displaying data that changes over a continuous range, typically representing time, to visualize trends, patterns, or fluctuations in the data.

Stacked Value Line Chart

In a stacked line chart, multiple lines are overlaid, and the values are stacked on top of each other, often representing the cumulative total.

Bar Charts

Ideal for representing and comparing distinct categories or discrete data points. It is an effective choice when you want to show the magnitude of different items or categories, allowing for quick visual comparisons and ranking.

Quicklinks

Normal Bar Chart

A visualization of each color-coded bar's association with the highest channel revenues, presenting quarterly variations in income for each respective channel.

Horizontal Bar Chart

In a horizontal bar chart, the bars are oriented horizontally, with the y-axis representing categories or labels, and the x-axis representing the values.

Well-suited for ranking or sorting data by value, with the longer bars indicating larger values, and it's often used in scenarios where the category labels are more descriptive.

Stacked Bar Chart

This chart is particularly useful when you want to display how different components contribute to the total, and it shows both the individual component values and their cumulative sum for each category or data point.

Scatter Plot Charts

Typically employed to visually examine the relationship between two continuous variables. It proves useful for identifying patterns, correlations, or outliers in the data. Each data point on the chart represents a single observation, and its position is determined by the values of the two variables under consideration.

The chart shows that a stronger subscriber connection leads to a higher level of likes, particularly when aiming for the highest likes target.

Pie Charts

Used to display data as a circle divided into segments, with each segment representing a proportion or percentage of a whole. It is suitable for illustrating the composition of a single dataset in a visually intuitive manner, where the size of each segment corresponds to its relative importance within the whole.

Representation of the number of channels for each category with each segment's distinct color and values providing clear insights into which categories are the most popular compared to the least favored.

Donut Charts

A donut chart is a circular graph that resembles a donut or ring. It conveys data by dividing the circle into segments, with each segment representing a category or variable. The central empty space gives it a ring-like appearance.

Single Value Donut Chart

A single value donut chart focuses on a single data point or category within the donut, with no additional rings or segments. It is typically used to highlight and emphasize the proportion or significance of one specific category in relation to the whole.

Representation of the top country with content creators in comparison to the rest of the world.

Area Charts

An area chart is used to display data that changes over time while emphasizing the cumulative total. It is particularly effective when illustrating how different components contribute to a whole and how they evolve in relation to each other.

Normal Area Chart

An area chart representing the subscriber growth of the top 5 YouTube channels over the past 5 years would show a series of filled areas, each corresponding to one of the top channels.

Stacked Area Chart

A stacked area chart represents multiple data series by overlapping areas, and the cumulative total is emphasized. Each area is stacked on top of the previous one, showing how the values of different data series contribute to the overall total.

Stat Chart

A stat chart is a visualization method designed to highlight a single data point for quick and clear understanding. It's particularly useful for summarizing aggregated data to provide meaningful insights.

Simple Stat Chart

The stat chart's orientation can be customized, allowing for various arrangements and layouts to be applied.
